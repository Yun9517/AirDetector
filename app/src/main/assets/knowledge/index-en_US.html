Total Volatile Organic Compounds(TVOC) are organic chemicals that have a high vapor pressure at ordinary room temperature.
        Their high vapor pressure results from a low boiling point(50~260 ℃), which causes large numbers of molecules to evaporate or sublimate from the liquid or solid form of the compound and enter the surrounding air, a trait known as volatility. Parts Per Billion(PPB) as standard measure unit.

        Outdoor sources include automobile exhaust, industrial processes, and wood burning. Indoors, TVOC may be released into the air by household or office products including personal care items, glue, air fresheners and printers. TVOC is also frequently produced from the off-gassing of building materials such as wood, carpet, and paint.
        The level of TVOC found indoors can vary significantly depending on the activities of the occupants and their use of products that emits TVOC.

        The health effects of TVOC can vary depending on the compound, concentration, and duration of exposure.
        Symptoms of exposure may include irritation of the eyes, nose, and throat, as well as nausea and difficulty breathing.
        Additionally, TVOC has been linked to chronic health conditions such as asthma, liver damage and central nervous system damage. Some are known or suspected carcinogens.

        According to German Federal Environmental Agency’s report, we can define different levels and exposure limit time.

        PM2.5 particles are air pollutants with a diameter of 2.5 micrometers or less, small enough to invade even the smallest airways. These particles generally come from activities that burn fossil fuels, such as traffic, smelting, and metal processing.


        PM2.5 can penetrate deeply into the lung, irritate and corrode the alveolar wall, and consequently impair lung function. Exposure to high concentrations of PM can also exacerbate lung and heart conditions, significantly affecting the quality of life, and increase deaths and hospital admissions.
        WHO suggests the PM2.5 24-hour mean exposure should not exceed 25 μg/m³.

<15 : Good
>=15, <35 : Moderate
>=35, <54 : High
>=54, <150 : Unhealthy
>=150, <250 : Harmful

        Relative humidity (RH) is the ratio of the partial pressure of water vapor to the equilibrium vapor pressure of water at a given temperature.
        In the weather forecast, it tells the probability of rain, fog, etc.
        Human feels much hotter in high relative humidity than the average because perspiration evaporates from the skin is slower than normal condition, also it may creates discomfort, mold, dust mites, skin allergies. Low humidity may causes thirsty and allergies, so do not forget to get some water.
        Normally, human feels cool and comfortable when the humidity percent range is about 45%~65% because they can't afford extreme dryness or moisture.
